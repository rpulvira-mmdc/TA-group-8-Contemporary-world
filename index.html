What is Globalization in Education?

Globalization connects classrooms worldwide through technology and shared learning systems. However, it also highlights global inequalities — especially as schools adopt AI and digital platforms at different paces.

The challenge: How can education remain inclusive and critical-thinking–driven in a world rapidly shaped by AI?

Artificial Intelligence as a Globalization Driver

AI transforms learning globally — from adaptive learning tools to automated assessments. But not all students and teachers benefit equally. Some lack access to digital tools, while others depend too heavily on AI, weakening human reasoning skills.

The goal: Promote responsible, equitable, and critical use of AI in education.

The Global Challenge

Unequal access to AI-powered tools between developed and developing nations.

Overreliance on AI, risking the decline of independent thinking.

Cultural and linguistic bias in AI systems that can distort learning materials.

AI Literacy and Critical Thinking Program

Trains students and teachers on responsible AI use.

Builds critical thinking, problem-solving, and ethics awareness.

Promotes inclusive access to AI learning tools.

SMART Goal

Layered Solutions to AI Integration
Multi-Level Approach to Preserving Critical Thinking

Our solution uses a layered, bottom-up approach to ensure responsible and meaningful AI use in education:

1. Institutional AI Usage Policies – Establish ethical frameworks and guidelines for responsible AI use.

2. AI Literacy & Critical Thinking Integration – Embed AI education and critical analysis into the curriculum.

3. Teacher-Mediated AI Use – Empower educators to guide students’ active, critical engagement with AI.

Solution 1: Institutional AI Usage Policies
Building the Foundation of Responsible AI Use

● Create official university or school AI-use policies that define ethical boundaries and acceptable practices.

● Encourage AI as a tool for learning, not as a replacement for thinking.

● Example: Mapúa Malayan Digital College, De La Salle University, and UP have released policies promoting trustworthy AI use.

Outcome: Builds a culture of academic honesty and meaningful engagement with AI.

Solution 2: AI Literacy and Critical Thinking Program
Bridging Policy and Practice

● Integrate AI literacy skills into General Education (GE) subjects.

● Focus on ethics, data interpretation, and critical reasoning when using AI.

● Supported by CHED’s National AI Roadmap (2025) aiming to produce 1M AI-literate workers by 2028.

Outcome: Students become adaptive, ethical, and critically aware AI users.

Solution 3: Teacher-Mediated AI Use
The Classroom as the Core of AI Transformation

● Teachers act as guides, not gatekeepers — facilitating meaningful AI integration in learning. Examples:

● Donnie Piercy (USA) – Students compared AI and human writing to enhance style and analysis.

● Jonathan Jones (University level) – Students critiqued AI essays to improve fact-checking and reasoning.

Outcome: Strengthens higher-order thinking, critical evaluation, and ethical engagement with AI.
